# UPI/Online Payment Fraud: The Case of Priya's Stolen Savings

UPI fraud involves scammers tricking victims into sharing sensitive details like UPI PINs or approving fake transactions, leading to unauthorized money transfers. It's rampant in India due to widespread use of apps like PhonePe, Google Pay, and Paytm.

## How it happens (step-by-step):

Bait: Victim gets a call or WhatsApp message pretending to be from their bank or UPI provider, claiming "suspicious activity" on their account.

Hook: Scammer asks for OTP or UPI PIN "to verify and secure" the account, or sends a fake payment request disguised as a refund.

Reel in: Victim shares details or taps "approve." Funds transfer instantly to scammer's account.

Cover-up: Scammer blocks victim and vanishes.

Priya, a 20-year-old engineering student in Bhopal, was targeted during exam season. She got a call from "PhonePe support" about a failed payment. Stressed, she shared her UPI PIN. Within minutes, ₹45,000 vanished from her account—her entire semester allowance.

Typical targets: College students, young professionals, and rural users new to digital payments, lured by urgency.

Consequences: Financial loss (often irreversible due to instant transfers), emotional distress, credit score damage, and legal hassles. Priya spent weeks recovering funds via police FIR, but lost trust in digital payments.
